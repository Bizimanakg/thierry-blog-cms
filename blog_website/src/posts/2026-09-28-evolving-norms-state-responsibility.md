The evolution of state responsibility in the context of human rights represents a critical shift in international public law. This commentary explores the expanding scope of positive obligations imposed on states by international tribunals.

## The Scope of Positive Obligations

Unlike negative obligations, which simply require the state to refrain from violating rights, positive obligations mandate proactive steps to protect individuals within the state's jurisdiction. This doctrine has increasingly been utilized to address systemic failures in governance and security.

> "A state's legitimacy is increasingly tethered to its capacity to affirmatively secure the human rights of its populace against both state and non-state actors."

International courts are continuously refining the standard of "due diligence" required by states. This standard is not absolute, yet ignorance or a lack of capacity no longer automatically shields a state from liability.

## Comparative Inquiry

By examining recent jurisprudence from regional human rights bodies, we observe a harmonization of standards regarding state omissions. The failure to investigate human rights abuses is now treated as a distinct and severe violation of international obligations.
